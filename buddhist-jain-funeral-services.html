Frequently Asked Questions

Buddhist Funeral Rites

What happens during a Buddhist funeral?

+

A Buddhist funeral typically involves prayers and chanting led by monks to help the consciousness of the deceased transition. The service is often simple, focusing on the teachings of impermanence. A viewing or wake may be held, followed by either cremation or burial.

Is cremation required in Buddhism?

+

Cremation is very common but not strictly required in all Buddhist traditions. The choice between burial and cremation often depends on the specific school of Buddhism and the family's personal preference. We can facilitate both.

Jain Funeral Rites

Why is a timely cremation important in Jainism?

+

In Jainism, it is customary to perform the cremation as soon as possible, ideally before sunset. This relates to the belief that the soul has already departed, and the body should be returned to its elemental state promptly and simply.

What specific rites do you follow for a Jain cremation?

+

We ensure that the process is simple and adheres to the core tenets of Jainism. This includes using a minimal amount of wood, avoiding the use of ghee to prevent harming insects, and ensuring the entire process is handled with respect and non-violence.
