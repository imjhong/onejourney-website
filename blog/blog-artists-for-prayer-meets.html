Arranging Artists for Prayer Meets: A Guide to Serene Tributes

By One Journey Team August 8, 2025 Memorial Services

Music holds a unique power to soothe the soul and create an atmosphere of peace and reflection. For post-funeral prayer meetings such as a Chautha, Tehravin, or a general memorial service, arranging for professional artists can be a beautiful way to honour a loved one and provide comfort to grieving guests.

Why Consider Professional Artists for a Prayer Meet?

While playing recorded music is an option, live devotional music offers a much more profound experience. Here are a few reasons why families choose to hire professional artists:

  • Creating a Divine Atmosphere: Skilled artists are adept at setting a serene, meditative tone that helps guests focus on prayers and remembrance.
  • Extensive Repertoire: Professionals have a wide range of bhajans, kirtans, shabads, and other devotional music suitable for the solemnity of the occasion.
  • Easing the Family's Burden: It removes the responsibility of managing music and sound systems from the grieving family.
  • Professionalism and Punctuality: You can be assured that the musical tribute will be conducted seamlessly, punctually, and with the utmost respect.

Types of Artists Available for Prayer Meetings

Depending on your faith and the desired atmosphere, several types of artists can be arranged:

  • Bhajan Singers: Ideal for Hindu prayer meetings, specializing in devotional songs dedicated to various deities.
  • Kirtani Jathas: For Sikh ceremonies, these groups perform Gurbani Kirtan, creating a deeply spiritual environment.
  • Instrumentalists: A solo harmonium player, tabla player, or a flutist can provide a more subtle and meditative background score.
  • Ghazal Singers: For some memorial services, the soulful and reflective nature of ghazals can be a fitting tribute.
A musical tribute is a heartfelt way to express devotion and celebrate the spiritual journey of the departed.
