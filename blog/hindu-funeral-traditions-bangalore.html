Hindu Funeral Traditions Followed in Bangalore

By One Journey Team August 8, 2025 Bangalore Services, Hindu Rituals

Bangalore, a city that beautifully blends modernity with tradition, is home to a diverse population, including many Hindu communities who uphold their sacred customs. The final rites, or Antim Sanskar, are performed with great reverence, combining ancient traditions with the practicalities of a metropolitan setting. This guide explains the key rituals and arrangements for a Hindu funeral in Bangalore.

Pre-Cremation Rituals in a Bangalore Home

The ceremonies begin at home, where the family prepares their loved one for their final journey.

  • The Final Bath (Antim Snan): The body is respectfully bathed, often with holy water from the Ganga, and anointed with traditional pastes like sandalwood.
  • Dressing (Vastra): The deceased is dressed in new, simple clothes. A married woman is often dressed in her wedding attire, while a man is dressed in a white dhoti or kurta.
  • Paying Last Respects: Family and community members visit to pay their last respects before the body is carried on a bier (arthi) to the crematorium.

The Cremation (Dah Sanskar) at a BBMP Facility

The final rites are conducted at one of Bangalore's designated crematoriums.

  • Booking and Paperwork: A professional funeral service in Bangalore will handle the booking at a BBMP crematorium and manage all the necessary documentation.
  • Pandit and Samagri: An experienced Pandit is essential to conduct the rites. All the required pooja samagri, from the earthen pot to sacred herbs, is arranged.
  • The Rituals: The chief mourner performs the final rites, including the Mukhagni, under the guidance of the pandit, as Vedic mantras are chanted to ensure peace for the soul.
In Bangalore, both electric and traditional wood pyre cremations are available. The choice often depends on family tradition and preference.

Post-Cremation Ceremonies: Vaikunta Samaradhane

The period of mourning and remembrance continues after the cremation.

  • Asthi Sanchay and Visarjan: The collection of ashes (asthi) is done on the third or fourth day. While Bangalore does not have a major holy river for immersion, families often travel to nearby sacred places like Srirangapatna (on the banks of the Kaveri) for the Asthi Visarjan.
  • Vaikunta Samaradhane: This is a significant post-funeral ceremony, usually held on the 13th day in Bangalore. It is a prayer meeting and community feast to honour the departed, mark the end of the mourning period, and pray for the soul's journey to Vaikuntha (heaven).
