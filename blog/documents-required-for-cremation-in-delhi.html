What Documents Are Required for Cremation in Delhi?

By One Journey Team August 8, 2025 Legal Formalities

Arranging a cremation in Delhi involves a series of legal and administrative steps that must be followed. While dealing with paperwork is the last thing on anyone's mind during a time of grief, having the right documents ready can make the process significantly smoother. This guide provides a clear checklist of the documents required for a cremation ceremony in Delhi.

The Most Crucial Document: The Death Certificate

No funeral rites can proceed without an official declaration of death by a qualified medical professional. This is the foundational document for the entire process.

  • If Death Occurs at a Hospital: The hospital administration is responsible for issuing the Medical Certificate of Cause of Death (MCCD), usually in Form 4 or 4A. This is the primary document you will need.
  • If Death Occurs at Home: You must immediately contact a registered medical practitioner (your family doctor or another qualified M.B.B.S. doctor). They will examine the deceased and issue the MCCD, stating the cause of death.

Essential Documents for the Cremation Ground

When you arrive at the cremation ground (Shamshan Ghat), you will need to present a set of documents to the authorities there to obtain a permit for the cremation.

  • Original Medical Certificate of Cause of Death (MCCD): The Form 4/4A issued by the doctor or hospital.
  • Identity Proof of the Deceased: A photocopy of an official ID like an Aadhaar Card, Voter ID, or Passport.
  • Identity Proof of the Informant: A photocopy of the ID of the close relative who is overseeing the cremation rituals.
  • Cremation Ground Form: A form provided by the crematorium that needs to be filled out with details of the deceased. A funeral service provider typically handles this for you.
"Having these documents organized beforehand can provide a great sense of relief, allowing the family to focus on the rituals and each other."

Special Circumstances

In certain situations, additional documentation is required by law.

  • Unnatural Death: In case of an accident, suicide, or any other unnatural death, a Post-Mortem Report and a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the local police station are mandatory before the cremation can take place.
  • Foreign Nationals: For foreign citizens who pass away in Delhi, an NOC from their respective embassy is required in addition to their passport and visa details.
