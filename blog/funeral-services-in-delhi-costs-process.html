Funeral Services in Delhi: Costs, Process & Support

By One Journey Team August 8, 2025 Funeral Planning

Arranging a funeral in a vast city like Delhi can be a deeply overwhelming task, especially when you are coping with the loss of a loved one. Understanding the process, potential costs, and the kind of support available can provide much-needed clarity. This guide aims to demystify funeral services in Delhi to help you make informed decisions.

Understanding the Costs of Funeral Services in Delhi

The cost of a funeral in Delhi can vary significantly based on the choices you make. There is no single price tag, but the total cost is typically determined by the following factors:

  • Type of Service: Cremation is more common for some faiths, while burial is mandated for others. The costs for each can differ.
  • Materials: The choice of a simple shroud, a basic coffin, or a more elaborate casket will impact the price.
  • Transportation: The type of vehicle (ambulance or decorated hearse) and the distance to be covered are key factors.
  • Ceremonies: Fees for priests (Pandits, Granthis, etc.), booking of prayer halls, and arrangement of samagri all contribute to the cost.
  • Crematorium Charges: Government-run facilities in Delhi have fixed, regulated charges for services like wood pyre or electric cremation.
For a clear, itemized quote based on your specific needs and traditions, it is always best to call a service provider directly for a transparent, no-obligation consultation.

The Step-by-Step Funeral Process in Delhi

While rituals vary by faith, the logistical process for arranging a funeral in Delhi generally follows these steps:

  • Step 1: First Call & Documentation: The immediate first step is to obtain a doctor's certificate for the cause of death. You can then call your funeral director, who will guide you on all documents required for cremation in Delhi.
  • Step 2: Transportation: The deceased is respectfully transported from the place of death (home or hospital) to the residence for final family rituals before proceeding to the cremation or burial ground.
  • Step 3: Planning the Ceremony: This involves deciding on the rituals based on your faith, be it a Hindu, Sikh, Islamic, Christian, or Secular service.
  • Step 4: The Final Rites: Your funeral director will manage all logistics at the crematorium (like Nigambodh Ghat or Lodhi Road) or the cemetery.
  • Step 5: Post-Ceremony Support: Arrangements are made for post-funeral gatherings, such as prayer meetings, Chautha/Tehravin, or memorial services.

Arranging a farewell in Delhi doesn't have to be a stressful experience. With the right support system, your family can focus on what truly matters: grieving, remembering, and honouring a life well-lived.
